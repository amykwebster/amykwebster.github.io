Amy K. Webster

PhD candidate in Genetics and Genomics at Duke University


I graduated with my B.S. in Genetics and Mathematics from the University of Georgia. I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the University Program in Genetics and Genomics (UPGG) at Duke University and an NSF Graduate Research Fellow, advised by Ryan Baugh. Broadly, I am interested in understanding how genetic and epigenetic factors interact to shape variation in quantitative traits that are important for an animal's fitness. Specifically, I study how ancestral diet, gene regulation, and natural genetic variation shape starvation resistance in the nematode C. elegans.
